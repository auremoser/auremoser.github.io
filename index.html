Hello! I am a curious web-cartographer and coder in Brooklyn, NYC. Right now, I work at the Mozilla Science Lab on open source projects and open science fellowships. I co-lead the NYC chapter of Girl Develop It, teach web-programming at Parsons-J+D and SVA-DSI, and give talks on geospatial tech at conferences.

Previously, I was a lab tech in libraries, a map scientist at Carto + Ushahidi, a sem-web radio show host, and a curator of curiosities at That Belongs in a Museum. Learn more in my CV.

Aure is part of a longer name that is both a jellyfish genus and a web-framework; I like science, scripting, and sobriquets.
