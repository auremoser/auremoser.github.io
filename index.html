I'm a curious web-cartographer, developer, and researcher living in Brooklyn, New York. I work at the Mozilla Science Lab, building projects and fellowship programs. Concurrently, I co-lead a pretty epic NY chapter of Girl Develop It, teach web-programming at Parsons-Journalism + Design and SVA-DSI, and give talks on geospatial tech at conferences.

Previously, I was a lab tech in libraries, a sem-web radio show host, and a curator of curiosities at That Belongs in a Museum.

Aure is part of a longer name that's both a jellyfish genus and a web-framework; I like science, scripting, and sobriquets.
